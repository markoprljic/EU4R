About EU4Reconstruction

A European Union flagship initiative rebuilding communities, restoring services, and strengthening governance across war-affected Ukraine.

Our Mission

Why EU4Reconstruction Exists

The war in Ukraine has caused unprecedented destruction to civilian infrastructure, housing, and public services. Millions of people have been displaced, and hundreds of communities face the daunting task of rebuilding — often with severely limited local resources and institutional capacity.

EU4Reconstruction was established in 2024 under the Ukraine Facility framework to channel targeted European Union funding and expertise directly to where it is most needed. The programme works in close coordination with the Ukrainian Ministry of Restoration, regional state administrations, and a network of international implementing partners.

Beyond physical reconstruction, the programme builds lasting institutional capacity — helping communities plan, procure, and manage reconstruction with transparency, efficiency, and long-term sustainability in mind.

🎯 Strategic Objective

Accelerate the recovery of war-affected Ukrainian communities by mobilising €1.2 billion in grants and technical assistance between 2024 and 2027.

🤝 Partnership Approach

Co-design and co-implementation with Ukrainian national and local authorities, ensuring national ownership and alignment with Ukraine's national recovery plan.

🌱 Build Back Better

All funded projects meet EU green and digital standards, ensuring that reconstruction contributes to long-term resilience, energy efficiency, and climate adaptation.

Funding Streams

How Funding is Structured

The programme distributes resources across four complementary streams, each addressing a distinct dimension of reconstruction.

Stream 1
🏠
Budget share
35%

Emergency Shelter & Housing

Addresses the most acute humanitarian need — restoring safe housing for families who have lost their homes or are living in damaged, unsafe structures. Funds cover rapid structural repair, full reconstruction of destroyed residential buildings, social housing construction, and temporary accommodation facilities.

Eligible applicants include Ukrainian municipalities, regional authorities, and housing cooperatives. Individual grant sizes range from €200k to €8M, with a simplified application track for grants under €500k.

Stream 2
🏗️
Budget share
30%

Critical Infrastructure

Targets the physical backbone of community life — roads, bridges, water and sanitation systems, schools, hospitals, and public administrative buildings. Restoration of these assets is a prerequisite for economic revival and the return of displaced populations.

Stream 2 includes a dedicated fast-track facility for critical water and sanitation repairs, with approval timelines of under 30 days for qualifying applications.

Stream 3
⚡
Budget share
25%

Energy Resilience

Rebuilds and modernises energy infrastructure with a focus on decentralisation and renewable integration. Supported activities include district heating system restoration, distributed solar and biomass installations, smart metering, and energy efficiency retrofits for public buildings.

A dedicated technical advisory service helps municipalities develop bankable energy transition project proposals aligned with REPowerEU principles.

Stream 4
🏛️
Budget share
10%

Municipal Capacity Building

Provides non-investment support to strengthen the institutions responsible for delivering reconstruction. Activities include training in EU-compliant procurement, financial management systems, GIS-based project planning tools, and peer-learning exchanges between Ukrainian and EU municipalities.

Stream 4 is delivered through a network of EU-based twinning municipalities and specialised technical assistance providers.

Governance

How the Programme is Governed

EU4Reconstruction operates under a multi-level governance framework that ensures transparency, accountability, and strong Ukrainian ownership throughout the programme cycle.

The Programme Steering Committee, co-chaired by the European Commission and the Ukrainian Ministry of Restoration, sets strategic direction and approves major funding decisions on a quarterly basis.

Download Governance Framework
🇪🇺 Programme Steering Committee

Co-chaired by DG NEAR and Ministry of Restoration. Sets strategy, approves budgets, monitors milestones.

🏢 Programme Management Unit

Day-to-day operations, grant management, and monitoring. Based in Kyiv with regional offices.

🔍 Independent Audit & Evaluation

Annual external audits and mid-term evaluation. Results published on this website.

🗣️ Civil Society Advisory Panel

Advisory panel of Ukrainian CSOs providing beneficiary feedback and social monitoring input.

Programme Timeline

Key Milestones

Q1 2024

Programme Launch

EU4Reconstruction formally established. Steering Committee constituted. Programme Management Unit operationalised in Kyiv.

Q2 2024

First Call for Proposals

Rounds 1 & 2 launched under Streams 1 and 2. 94 applications received; 28 grants awarded totalling €187M.

Q4 2024

Stream 3 Opens

Energy Resilience stream activated. 62 proposals received. First winter season support packages deployed.

Q2 2025 — Current

Round 4 Call Now Open

Largest single call to date: €320M available across Streams 1–3. Deadline 15 May 2025.

2027

Programme Completion

All grants fully disbursed, final evaluation completed, and programme results report published.
