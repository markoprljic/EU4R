EU and Ukraine Sign €480M Additional Commitment for 2025

The European Commission has confirmed a significant expansion of the EU4Reconstruction programme, with an additional €480 million allocation formally signed at the EU-Ukraine Summit in Brussels on 10 March 2025. The agreement was signed by Commissioner for International Partnerships Jutta Urpilainen and Ukrainian Deputy Prime Minister for European and Euro-Atlantic Integration Olha Stefanishyna.

Expanding Coverage to New Oblasts

The additional funding will extend EU4Reconstruction's geographic reach to three newly stabilised oblasts — Kherson, Donetsk, and Luhansk — where the scale of infrastructure destruction and humanitarian need is among the highest in the country. An initial assessment conducted by the Programme Management Unit in late 2024 identified over 1,400 priority reconstruction sites across the three oblasts, covering residential buildings, water and sanitation systems, schools, hospitals, and energy infrastructure.

"This additional commitment reflects the European Union's unwavering solidarity with the people of Ukraine and our shared determination to ensure that every community — however affected — has access to the resources needed to rebuild," said Commissioner Urpilainen at the signing ceremony.

Accelerated Timelines and Simplified Procedures

Alongside the expanded budget, the agreement introduces a number of procedural improvements aimed at accelerating grant delivery. These include a new fast-track pathway for emergency housing repairs — with approval timelines cut from 90 to 21 days — and a simplified financial reporting framework for grants under €1 million, reducing administrative burden on smaller municipalities and civil society organisations.

The programme will also pilot a new performance-based disbursement model for three major infrastructure projects, where a portion of funding is released upon verified completion of pre-agreed milestones. This approach, developed in consultation with the European Court of Auditors, is designed to strengthen accountability while maintaining implementation speed.

Implications for Round 4 Call

The additional allocation significantly increases the budget available under Round 4 of the Call for Proposals, which was launched in February 2025. Applicants from Kherson, Donetsk, and Luhansk oblasts are now eligible to apply under all three investment streams, with a dedicated reserve of €120 million for projects in these regions.

The deadline for Round 4 applications remains 15 May 2025. Eligible applicants are encouraged to attend the online information sessions scheduled for 25 March and 8 April 2025.

Next Steps

The Programme Management Unit will publish updated application guidelines, regional eligibility maps, and a revised FAQ document within two weeks of this announcement. Prospective applicants from the three newly eligible oblasts are encouraged to register their interest via the contact form on this website to receive priority communication about the opening of the regional application window.
