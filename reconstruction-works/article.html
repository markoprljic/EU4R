Kharkiv Multi-Family Housing Repair — Saltivka District

Project ID: EU-KHARK-24-001 · Started October 2024 · Expected completion September 2025

This project addresses the critical housing needs of families in Saltivka, one of Kharkiv's most densely populated residential districts and among the most severely damaged by shelling in 2022–2023. At its peak, over 80% of buildings in the district sustained damage, leaving thousands of families without safe shelter.

Project Overview

The EU4Reconstruction grant of €4.8 million funds emergency structural repair and essential services restoration across 18 multi-storey residential buildings in the Saltivka district of Kharkiv. Works are being implemented by the Kharkiv City Council Department of Housing and Municipal Infrastructure in partnership with local contractors certified under EU procurement standards.

The project targets buildings with moderate-to-severe structural damage that are fundamentally repairable, as assessed by structural engineers from the NGO Rebuilding Ukraine and confirmed by an independent EU technical team. Buildings with irreparable damage are excluded from this project and are being addressed under a separate new-build programme.

Scope of Works

Each of the 18 buildings is receiving the following repairs, tailored to its specific condition assessment:

  • Structural stabilisation of damaged load-bearing walls and columns
  • Full roof replacement with insulated membrane roofing system
  • Façade repair and external wall insulation (meeting EU energy performance standards)
  • Window and external door replacement (triple-glazed units)
  • Stairwell and common area restoration
  • Electrical system upgrade and emergency lighting installation
  • Central heating system repair and thermostatic controls

Beneficiaries and Community Impact

When complete, the project will provide safe, warm housing to approximately 1,200 families — some 3,200 individuals — who have been either displaced or living in unsafe conditions. A community liaison officer has been appointed to maintain regular communication with residents throughout the construction period.

The project includes specific provisions for accessibility: all repaired buildings will have ramp access to ground floor entrances, and lifts will be restored or upgraded in buildings of six storeys or more, in compliance with Ukrainian building codes and EU accessibility standards.

Monitoring and Verification

The Programme Management Unit conducts monthly site visits to verify progress against the approved work schedule. An independent verification engineer from KfW Development Bank provides quarterly milestone assessments. All disbursements are contingent on satisfactory completion of agreed work packages, as verified by the independent engineer.

Project data, including financial reporting and site inspection reports, are available upon request to registered stakeholders through the programme's grant management portal.
