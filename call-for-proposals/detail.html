Round 4 Call for Proposals

€320 million available for reconstruction projects across Ukraine. Deadline: 15 May 2025.

Application deadline 15 May 2025

Overview

Round 4 is the largest and most comprehensive call for proposals launched by EU4Reconstruction to date. With €320 million available across Streams 1, 2, and 3, it represents a major acceleration of the programme's investment in Ukraine's recovery and rebuilding.

Following the signature of the March 2025 amendment agreement between the European Commission and the Ukrainian government, Round 4 has been extended to include Kherson, Donetsk, and Luhansk oblasts, with a dedicated regional reserve of €120 million for these newly eligible areas.

Eligible Priorities

Applications must target one of the following priority areas:

Stream 1 — Emergency Shelter & Housing

  • Structural repair of damaged multi-apartment residential buildings
  • Full reconstruction of destroyed residential buildings
  • Accessible social housing for displaced families with disabilities
  • Temporary accommodation facilities with a transition plan to permanent housing

Stream 2 — Critical Infrastructure

  • Repair or reconstruction of roads, bridges, and culverts
  • Rehabilitation of water supply and sanitation systems
  • Reconstruction of schools, hospitals, and community facilities
  • Repair of solid waste management infrastructure

Stream 3 — Energy Resilience

  • District heating system restoration and modernisation
  • Solar PV and battery storage systems for public buildings
  • Smart metering and grid management systems
  • Energy efficiency retrofits for residential and public buildings

Evaluation Criteria

Applications are evaluated on the following criteria, weighted as shown:

  • Relevance and needs assessment (25%) — Quality of the problem analysis and alignment with identified priority needs
  • Technical quality and feasibility (30%) — Soundness of the technical design and evidence of implementation capacity
  • Value for money (20%) — Cost-effectiveness and efficiency of the proposed approach
  • Sustainability (15%) — Credibility of long-term operation and maintenance arrangements
  • Cross-cutting standards (10%) — Gender equality, disability inclusion, environmental safeguards, and anti-corruption measures
